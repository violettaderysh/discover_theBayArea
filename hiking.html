The Best Hiking Trails in the Bay Area

The San Francisco Bay Area: A Hiker's Paradise

The San Francisco Bay Area is a hiker's paradise, offering an incredible variety of trails that showcase the region’s natural beauty. Whether you’re looking for a peaceful stroll through towering redwood forests, a breezy walk across the iconic Golden Gate Bridge, or a challenging climb with sweeping views, the Bay Area has it all.

Nature enthusiasts can admire vibrant wildflowers in bloom, while beginners can enjoy trails that strike the perfect balance between effort and reward. From scenic coastal paths to rugged mountain adventures, these trails cater to all skill levels and promise unforgettable views at every turn.

Find Your Trail

Lands End Trail

A coastal trail with stunning views of the Golden Gate Bridge and rugged cliffs.

  • Length: 3.4 miles (1.5 - 2 hours)
  • Route type: Loop
  • Difficulty Level: Moderate
  • Dog-Friendly: Yes (on leash)
  • Wheelchair-friendly: No

Muir Woods Main Trail

A peaceful walk through towering redwoods in a pristine forest setting.

  • Length: 31.5 miles (0.5 - 1 hour)
  • Route type: Out & back
  • Difficulty Level: Easy
  • Dog-Friendly: No
  • Wheelchair-friendly: Yes

Mission Peak Trail

A steep hike leading to panoramic views of the Bay Area, ideal for experienced hikers.

  • Length: 5.8 miles (4–5 hours)
  • Route type: Out & back
  • Difficulty Level: Hard
  • Dog-Friendly: Yes (on leash)
  • Wheelchair-friendly: No
